      Indicator Rubric (1-4)

      Quick Reference

      Visible Scholar Thinking

      • 1: Evidence: work shows answers only; no annotations or exit tickets collected. Action: students cannot explain their reasoning without prompts.
      • 2: Evidence: partial notes or occasional talk moves captured in samples. Action: students explain steps inconsistently or leave reasoning unfinished.
      • 3: Evidence: consistent annotated work, error analysis, or conferencing notes collected. Action: students explain reasoning with clear steps and representations.
      • 4: Evidence: multi-source artifacts over time show growth (work samples, exit tickets, talk moves). Action: students independently justify, compare strategies, and revise using evidence.

      Problem Solving Mastery

      • 1: Evidence: work shows misread prompts and incomplete steps. Action: students guess or start without selecting a strategy.
      • 2: Evidence: some steps recorded but strategy is mismatched or errors remain. Action: students identify the task with prompts and apply a strategy inconsistently.
      • 3: Evidence: recorded steps show correct strategy and accurate solution. Action: students explain how the strategy fits the question and check their work.
      • 4: Evidence: work samples show strategy choice, checks, and revisions across tasks. Action: students adapt strategies independently and reflect on learning.

      Goal Agency

      • 1: Evidence: no tracked data or goal sheet present. Action: students cannot state current scores or next steps.
      • 2: Evidence: goal sheets exist but updates are sporadic. Action: students state a goal with prompts and follow a teacher-directed plan.
      • 3: Evidence: data trackers show regular updates and checkpoints. Action: students state current data, goal, and explain their plan.
      • 4: Evidence: progress monitoring logs show adjustments after checks. Action: students lead data conversations, adjust plans, and articulate next steps.
